How to manage and organise your music in iTunes

I use iTunes which is the best stand alone application. It works whether you are connected to the internet or not to organize and manage your music. Some people perfer other one's like Windows Media, Winamp, MediaMonkey, Tuneup Media and other well known applications.
iTunes is easy and layed out for me to play, rip, import, delete, search, rip, and add the artwork to each artist. You can rip music from a CD and import it into iTunes, thus copying songs from your CDs to your iTunes library. Then sync the ripped songs to your iPod.



Add items from your computer to your iTunes library


  • Drag a file or folder from the desktop to the iTunes window.
  • In iTunes, choose File > Add to Library, locate a file or folder, and click Open.
  • It automatically transfer music and places a copy of each audio file in the iTunes folder (Home/Music/iTunes) and leaves the original file in its current location

Import songs from CDs in iTunes


  • Insert an audio CD into your computer’s CD or DVD drive, or an external drive that’s connected to your computer.
  • Click Yes in the window that appears. iTunes starts importing the CD.
  • When the list of songs appears in the iTunes window, click to remove the checkmark next to any songs you don’t want to import.

How to rip Music from CDs into iTunes


  • Insert an audio CD into your computer.
  • iTunes automatically rips and retrieves the track information if connected to the internet.
  • Once the list of songs are ripped to iTunes window, click to remove the checkmark next to any songs you don’t want.
